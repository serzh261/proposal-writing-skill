# Agency Pattern — Federal Civilian (HHS, GSA, EPA, DOT)

For bids with federal civilian agencies including Department of Health and Human Services, General Services Administration, Environmental Protection Agency, Department of Transportation, and similar civilian agencies.

**Important context for [Firm]:** As of the current state, [Firm] has no completed federal primes, no facility clearance, no DoD/IC past performance. The federal civilian space is the realistic entry point — not DoD, not IC. And entry is more likely via sub-pathway than prime initially, until federal past performance accumulates.

## Buyer profile (general)

Federal civilian agencies:
- Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) governs procurement
- Larger budgets and longer contract values than state/local
- Multi-volume proposal structures (cover letter, technical, cost/price, reps & certs, sometimes management plan and past performance separately)
- Source selection by formal evaluation factors (Section M)
- Past performance evaluated via CPARS for prior federal contractors
- Set-asides for small business / 8(a) / WOSB / HUBZone often determine which firms can prime
- Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization (OSDBU) at each agency manages small biz relationships

## What they value

1. **FAR compliance.** Mandatory. Section L formatting requirements are strict — page limits, font, spacing all enforced. Misses = disqualification.

2. **Past performance evidence.** CPARS ratings for federal contractors, or strong commercial/state past performance with verifiable references. The [Federal Intermediary] $[REDACTED]M federal program is [Firm]'s strongest anchor here.

3. **Section M evaluation factor alignment.** Win themes must anchor to specific evaluation factors. Generic "we're great at X" loses to "Section M Factor 2 is best served by our Y because Z."

4. **Quantified value.** Dollar savings, defect rate improvements, on-time completion percentages. Numbers beat adjectives.

5. **Risk management.** Federal evaluators discount aggressive promises and reward realistic risk identification with concrete mitigation plans.

6. **Small business participation.** For unrestricted competitions, large primes need to demonstrate small business utilization. For set-asides, only qualifying small firms can prime.

7. **Citation accuracy.** FAR clause references, CAS citations, regulatory references — all must be current and accurate.

## What they don't care about (or value less)

- Marketing language and adjectives
- Brand-name partnerships without substance
- Local presence (federal civilian work is national)
- Two-week turnaround claims (they expect proposals to take 6-12 weeks)

## Federal proposal structure

Federal civilian proposals are typically multi-volume. Load `references/federal-rfp-scaffolding.md` for the FAR-aligned full structure (adapted from CaseMark Apache 2.0 — see attribution there).

Standard volumes:

1. **Cover Letter** (≤2 pages) — addressed to named CO. Solicitation reference. Validity period (typically 60-120 days). Authorized signatory.

2. **Executive Summary** (≤3 pages) — restate requirements interpretively. Connect SOW to agency mission. Name discriminators.

3. **Technical Approach** — mirror SOW structure. One response per major task. Methods, tools, standards, QA/QC, risks with mitigation, diagrams with action captions.

4. **Management Plan** — org structure, key personnel resumes, schedule, administration, staffing, succession.

5. **Past Performance** — 3-5 projects with CPARS ratings (if available) or verifiable references. Quantified outcomes.

6. **Cost/Price Breakdown** — by government cost element: direct labor (LCAT, hours, rates), fringe/overhead/G&A, materials, subcontracts, travel, ODCs, fee/profit.

7. **Cost Supporting Documentation** — basis of estimate, labor rate documentation, vendor quotes, indirect rate documentation, escalation factor support.

8. **Representations and Certifications** — SAM.gov status, small business size, tax compliance, executive compensation disclosure, OCI disclosures.

## Pricing benchmarks

- Federal civilian small business contracts — typical range $100K-$2M depending on scope
- [Firm]'s likely entry point — $250K-$750K small business set-aside or 8(a) sole-source
- Larger federal civilian work ($1M+) more realistic via teaming as sub to a federal prime initially

Rate structure:
- Direct labor by LCAT (labor category) with loaded and unloaded rates
- Fringe ~25-30%, overhead ~25-40%, G&A ~10-20% typical
- Profit 5-10% on cost-type, higher on FFP

For [Firm] as a small firm without formal indirect rates established, expect to negotiate. Pre-Award Indirect Rate Agreement (PIRA) or rate build-up with documented basis.

## Submission specifics

- SAM.gov entity registration must be current ([Firm]'s was pending activation as of last update — confirm before federal bid effort)
- Submission via Beta.SAM.gov, agency-specific portals, or email per RFP
- Strict deadlines, often by minute (e.g., 2:00:00 PM Eastern)
- Multi-volume submission means multiple files, named per RFP convention
- Page limits enforced strictly

## After federal civilian win — pattern continuation

- CPARS rating (if it accumulates over the period of performance)
- Agency-specific OSDBU relationship for future opportunities at that agency
- Vehicle access (some federal contracts grant access to GSA Schedules, IDIQ vehicles)

## Anti-patterns specific to federal civilian bids

- **Don't pursue clearance-required work as prime.** No facility clearance = no-go.
- **Don't assume small business set-asides without verifying.** Some RFPs are unrestricted competitions where [Firm] would compete against large primes. Verify the set-aside designation in the RFP.
- **Don't price too aggressively.** Federal evaluators see suspiciously low pricing as inability to deliver. Federal rate structures with proper indirect loads typically produce $200-300/hr blended rates, not the $[REDACTED]/hr [Firm] uses for state/regional.
- **Don't promise CPARS ratings you don't have.** If [Firm] has no federal past performance, lead with strongest adjacent ([Federal Intermediary] $[REDACTED]M federal funder + [Federal Grantee] federal grantee). Acknowledge [Firm] hasn't held federal primes yet but frame the adjacent experience as the relevant capability.
- **Don't skip reps & certs.** False certifications trigger termination and debarment.
- **Don't use marketing language.** Every claim must cite evidence (past performance, test results, certifications). Federal evaluators discount unsupported claims.
- **Don't paraphrase agency mission language.** Mirror their strategic plan and mission verbatim where relevant.
