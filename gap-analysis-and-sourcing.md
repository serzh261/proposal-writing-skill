# Gap Analysis and Sourcing Playbooks

Use these playbooks when gap analysis (`modes/gap-analysis.md`) identifies a hire is needed. Each playbook covers where to source, what to look for, and an outreach template.

The [GIS Subcontractor] precedent (Upwork, [GIS-certified] GIS subcontractor, $[REDACTED]/hr) is the proof these playbooks work. Don't be afraid to source aggressively.

## Playbook 1 — GIS / ESRI Specialist (Upwork)

**Proven path. [GIS Subcontractor] was sourced this way.**

**Where to search:**
- Upwork: https://www.upwork.com/freelancers
- Search terms: "ArcGIS ESRI consultant", "GIS analyst", "ESRI certified"
- Filters: Top Rated, US-based, available, $50-100/hr range

**What to look for:**
- ESRI certifications (Modern Geo Apps, Spatial Analyst, ArcGIS Pro Professional)
- Job Success Score 90%+
- 30+ completed projects
- Government or infrastructure project experience in profile
- Hourly rate $50-85 typical for solid mid-tier; below $50 likely undersized for federal/state work
- Active in last 30 days

**Outreach template:**

```
Subject: GIS Subcontractor — [Brief project description]

Hi [Name],

I lead [Firm Name], a [Metro Area]-area analytics firm. We're 
preparing a bid for [generic scope description — don't name the buyer until 
you have a verbal commit] that includes GIS / ESRI scope.

I'd be interested in talking about a subcontractor engagement. The GIS portion 
would run roughly [X-Y hours] over [Z weeks/months]. We'd want you named in our 
proposal as the GIS lead, with a short bio attached.

Your [specific credential — e.g., "Modern Geo Apps certification"] and your 
government project experience look like a strong fit.

Quick questions before we go further:
1. Available in the [date range] timeframe?
2. Rate?
3. Comfortable being named in a government contract proposal?

If interested, I can send more detail on the scope. Happy to chat by phone or 
keep this on Upwork — your preference.

— [Principal Name]
[Firm Name]
[contact info]
```

**Timeline:** 1-3 days from message to first response, 5-10 days to verbal commit.

**Cost ballpark:** $58-75/hr × 100-200 hours = $5,800-$15,000 per typical bid.

## Playbook 2 — Federal Grant Writer (Upwork or LinkedIn)

**Where to search:**
- Upwork: search "federal proposal writer", "GovCon proposal manager", "RFP response writer"
- LinkedIn: search "Federal Proposal Manager" + filter by location and current/past employer (looking for APMP, Shipley, or large-prime experience)
- APMP Member Directory (https://www.apmp.org)

**What to look for:**
- APMP certification (Foundation / Practitioner / Professional)
- Prior work writing proposals for the specific agency type (federal civilian vs. DoD vs. higher-ed)
- Has written at scale (10+ federal proposals)
- Has worked on small-business or 8(a) proposals (different writing style than large primes)

**Outreach template:**

```
Subject: Proposal writer for [federal civilian / state] bid — [due date]

Hi [Name],

I'm pursuing a [type] bid with proposal due [date]. I need a writer who's done 
this kind of solicitation before to help me produce a compliant, evaluation-ready 
response.

The opportunity is [generic description]. Estimated proposal effort: [X hours]. 
Compliance matrix and technical content I'll own; I want you on structure, 
clarity, win theme articulation, and compliance verification.

Your APMP background and prior work on [specific past work] looks like a fit. 
Quick questions:

1. Available [date range]?
2. Rate (hourly or fixed for the full bid)?
3. Willing to sign an NDA for the specifics?

— [Principal Name]
[Firm Name]
```

**Timeline:** 2-5 days to first response.
**Cost ballpark:** $100-200/hr × 20-40 hours = $2,000-$8,000.

## Playbook 3 — Domain SME (LinkedIn or Network)

For SMEs in specific domains (clinical, customs/trade, workforce development, environmental, transportation planning).

**Where to search:**
- LinkedIn search: "[domain] consultant" + location
- LinkedIn search: "retired [agency]" — retired federal staff make excellent SMEs
- Professional associations specific to the domain (NAEMT for emergency medicine, AACE for cost engineering, NICE for cybersecurity workforce)
- University extension / consulting offices for academic SMEs

**What to look for:**
- 15+ years in the domain
- Recent federal or state government work (within last 5 years)
- Written/published in the domain (signals credibility for proposal bios)
- Available for consulting (not full-time-employed without consent)

**Outreach template:**

```
Subject: [Domain] SME consultant — proposal opportunity

Hi [Name],

I'm preparing a [agency type] bid that includes [specific domain scope]. I'm 
looking for a domain SME to:

- Provide subject-matter input on the technical approach
- Be named as a SME on the proposal (with bio attached)
- Available for clarification calls with the buyer if requested
- Optionally take a small delivery role if we win

The bid is due [date]. SME effort would be roughly [X hours] of input writing 
and review before submission, then potentially [Y hours/week] post-award.

Your background — particularly [specific credential or past role] — is exactly 
the kind of credibility this proposal needs. Quick questions:

1. Interested in principle?
2. Available before [date]?
3. Rate?

Happy to set up a 20-minute call this week.

— [Principal Name]
[Firm Name]
```

**Timeline:** 5-14 days. SMEs are slower than freelancers.
**Cost ballpark:** $150-400/hr × 10-30 hours = $1,500-$12,000.

## Playbook 4 — Teaming as Subcontractor (Outreach to Primes)

When gap analysis says "can't prime this, sub instead." Approach 2-3 prime contractors to offer [Firm] as a sub.

**Where to find primes:**
- SAM.gov: search by NAICS + agency for current prime contract holders
- USASpending.gov: top-N contract recipients at the target agency
- The agency's small business specialist office (every federal agency has one) — they keep lists of primes seeking small business subs
- Industry-day attendee lists (if published)
- Sources sought / RFI responses (often public)

**Targets:**
- Large primes with subcontracting plans (required at federal level)
- Mid-tier primes who may bid this work and need small-business participation to meet their plan goals
- Niche primes in the same NAICS who could use [Firm]'s Microsoft analytics depth

**Outreach template:**

```
Subject: Small business subcontractor — data analytics — [Solicitation #]

Hi [Name — typically BD Director or Small Business Liaison],

I lead [Firm Name], a small business (NAICS 541611/541512/541690), 
KC-area firm. I'm reaching out about [Solicitation Number, Title], due [date].

If you're bidding as prime, I'd like to discuss subcontracting [Firm]'s 
data analytics capability for the [specific scope element]. [Firm]'s relevant 
past performance includes:

- [CCAR 1 — one line]
- [CCAR 3 — one line]

We're Microsoft-native (Power BI, Azure, SharePoint, Fabric), with ESRI/GIS via 
a named subcontractor ([GIS Subcontractor], Modern Geo Apps certified). We have no federal 
prime experience yet, which is why we're approaching teaming rather than priming.

A one-page capability statement is attached. If you'd like a 30-minute discussion 
or a more detailed scoping conversation, let me know.

— [Principal Name]
[Firm Name]
[contact]
```

**Timeline:** 3-10 days to first response from a prime BD team. Faster if you've worked with them before or have an internal champion.

**Cost:** Zero out-of-pocket. The prime owns proposal cost; [Firm] provides capability statement and bio.

**Strategic note:** Even if the prime doesn't bid this opportunity or doesn't pick [Firm] as a sub for it, you've introduced [Firm] to a new prime. That builds the rolodex for future bids.

## Playbook 5 — Graphics / Designer (Upwork)

For proposals where visual quality matters (federal proposals, executive-audience higher-ed proposals).

**Where to search:**
- Upwork: "proposal graphics" or "infographic designer" or "technical illustrator"
- 99designs, Behance for portfolio review

**What to look for:**
- Sample work on technical or government proposals
- InDesign or Figma proficiency
- Ability to work in Microsoft Word (where [Firm] proposals live)
- Quick turnaround (small firms need this in days, not weeks)

**Outreach template:** Similar to Playbook 1 (GIS) — short, specific scope, named role, fast turnaround.

**Cost ballpark:** $75-100/hr × 8-12 hours = $600-$1,200.

## When NOT to hire — alternatives

### Alternative 1: Reframe the scope

Sometimes the right move is to scope-reduce the proposal so the gap isn't material. Example: an RFP asks for Section 508 accessibility audit + dashboard. If [Firm] can build the dashboard with 508 best practices baked in but can't do a formal audit, propose "Section 508-compliant dashboard built to WCAG 2.1 AA standards" without offering a separate audit. The buyer may not need a formal audit.

### Alternative 2: Stack named subcontractors

Instead of hiring an SME, name an academic or industry consultant who has agreed to be available *if needed* (a letter of intent / availability rather than a full subcontract). This works for low-effort SME advisory roles.

### Alternative 3: Defer the capability to Phase 2

Many RFPs span multiple phases. Some capabilities are needed in Phase 2 or beyond. If Phase 1 is doable with [Firm]'s bench, propose Phase 2 capability through a future hire or sub conditional on award. Buyers accept this as long as the plan is concrete.

### Alternative 4: Walk away

The hiring-to-bid framework's role is to honestly identify when walking away is the right move. Pursuing a bid that requires structural gaps you can't fill is a money-losing strategy.

## After the bid

Track every sourced hire in your personal records (not in this skill — [Principal] has explicitly chosen not to maintain a persistent bench file). For each:
- Name, contact, rate, certification, scope coverage
- Whether they were named in a bid
- Whether the bid won
- Their performance if engaged

This builds [Principal]'s informal rolodex and shortens future sourcing cycles. The next time a similar gap appears, the first call is to someone [Firm] already knows.
