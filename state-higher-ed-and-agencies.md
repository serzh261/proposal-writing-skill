# Agency Pattern — State University System & State Agency Bids

> **Public-portfolio note:** This file is a sanitized template. The original encodes specific patterns for one state university system and state agencies. The structure here is reusable; fill in the specifics for your own market.

State universities and state agencies are a distinct buyer class from federal civilian or regional councils. Different procurement portals, different evaluation patterns, different price elasticity.

## Buyer profile

State universities typically have:
- An internal data/analytics office (sometimes branded like "Institutional Research" or "Analytics & Decision Support")
- An existing measurement framework with named products (often legacy reports or dashboards everyone references)
- Multi-campus or multi-unit structure requiring coordination
- A stated technology direction (often Microsoft Fabric, Snowflake, or Tableau Server)
- Procurement run through a state portal, not direct contracting

State agencies typically have:
- More rigid procurement rules than universities
- Legislative reporting requirements driving the work
- Lower price elasticity than federal but higher than regional councils
- A statewide procurement portal (each state has one — research yours)

## What they value

1. **Understanding of their measurement framework.** Naming their legacy products, frameworks, and reporting structures is a depth signal that costs nothing and pays a lot.
2. **Multi-unit coordination experience.** They need someone who can work across campuses, departments, or jurisdictions without escalating every disagreement.
3. **Microsoft-native or stack-aligned tooling.** If they've signaled a direction, align to it. Don't pitch Tableau if they're going Power BI.
4. **Realistic price discipline.** State budgets are tight. Conservative fixed-fee pricing beats hourly with risk premium.

## What gets you disqualified

- Generic "we work with universities" pitch with no reference to *their* institution
- Pitching tools they don't use
- Pricing structures that look like federal blended rates ($200-300/hr)
- Missing portal-specific responses (most state portals require BOTH a PDF and portal-form responses; some bidders only do the PDF)

## Pricing benchmarks

State university analytics engagements (3-6 month scope, single workstream): typically lower than federal equivalents and higher than small regional council work. Calibrate to your market — fixed-fee preferred over T&M.

## Portal mechanics

Most state procurement portals have:
- A main PDF response attachment
- Portal-form responses scored separately (often called "Attributes" or "Line Items")
- Pricing entered directly in the portal, not just in the PDF
- File size limits and naming conventions

**Don't only do the PDF.** Bidders who skip the portal-form responses get scored on incomplete submissions.

## Win theme patterns that work

**Theme: Evolution, not replacement.** State buyers have invested years in their existing frameworks. Position your work as continuing their direction, not throwing it out. Reference their legacy products by name.

**Theme: Microsoft-native stack alignment.** If the buyer has signaled Fabric / Power BI / Azure, lean into the alignment. This is a discriminator against agency-shop bidders who pitch their stack rather than the buyer's.

**Theme: Local presence (when applicable).** State universities often prefer in-state or near-state vendors for site-visit feasibility. Mention proximity when it's true.

## Proposal structure typical for state higher-ed bids

| Section | Pages | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cover Letter | 1 | Signed by principal |
| Executive Summary | 1 | Lead with buyer's problem |
| Understanding of Need | 1 | Reference their measurement framework by name |
| Technical Approach | 2-3 | Phased delivery, stack-aligned |
| Team & Roles | 1.5 | Principal + named subs |
| Past Performance | 1.5 | Three references, verified |
| Pricing Summary | 0.5 | Portal carries detail |

Total: 8-10 pages plus appendices and portal-form responses.

## Common mistakes

- Submitting only the PDF and ignoring portal-form responses (Attributes, Line Items)
- Generic past performance without "Relevance to [Buyer]:" callouts
- Pricing at federal blended rates
- No reference to the buyer's existing measurement framework
- Missing portal-specific deadlines (Q&A windows, registration confirmations)
