# Template — Sourcing Outreach Messages

Outreach message templates for the five sourcing playbooks in `references/gap-analysis-and-sourcing.md`. Adapt each to the specific opportunity and candidate.

These follow the [GIS Subcontractor] pattern that worked: short, specific scope, clear ask, three direct questions, no marketing fluff.

---

I'd be interested in talking about a subcontractor engagement. The GIS portion would run roughly [X-Y hours] over [Z weeks/months]. We'd want you named in our proposal as the GIS lead, with a short bio attached.

Your [specific credential — e.g., "Modern Geo Apps certification"] and your government project experience look like a strong fit.

Quick questions before we go further:
1. Available in the [date range] timeframe?
2. Rate?
3. Comfortable being named in a government contract proposal?

If interested, I can send more detail on the scope. Happy to chat by phone or keep this on Upwork — your preference.

— [Principal Name]
[Firm Name]
[email] | [phone]

## Customization checklist before sending

For every outreach message:

- [ ] Recipient's name is correct and spelled correctly
- [ ] Reference to their specific credential, project, or background is real (not generic)
- [ ] Date and timeframe are accurate
- [ ] Generic scope description (no buyer name leak before verbal commit)
- [ ] Three questions are answerable in 1-2 sentences each
- [ ] No banned phrases or AI-tells
- [ ] [Principal]'s contact info is included
- [ ] Subject line is specific (not "Quick question" or similar)

## Follow-up cadence

If no response in [days]:

- Day 3-5: gentle follow-up referencing the original message
- Day 7-10: brief "still relevant, here's the deadline" note
- After day 10: move on; the candidate is unavailable or uninterested

Don't pursue beyond 2-3 follow-ups. Quality candidates respond promptly; non-responsive candidates aren't worth chasing on tight proposal timelines.

## Tracking

Maintain personal records (not in this skill — [Principal] has opted not to keep a persistent bench file) of every outreach:

- Candidate name, channel, date contacted
- Response (or lack thereof)
- Rate quoted (if any)
- Decision (named in bid / kept as backup / pass)
- Performance (if engaged)

This builds the informal rolodex and shortens future sourcing cycles.
