# Mode: Reviews

Color team reviews are formal proposal-quality checks at defined milestones. Standard Shipley practice uses three (Pink for compliance, Red for content, Gold for executive polish). For a solo shop like [Firm], two are enough — Pink overlaps too much with the compliance matrix and is too early-stage to add value.

Use this mode when [Principal] says "review my proposal," "red team review," "gold team review," or has a near-final draft and wants pressure-testing.

## Red Team Review — content and persuasion

**When:** Draft is complete and roughly 60-70% of the way to submission. Significant rewrites are still possible.

**Question this review answers:** *"Would an evaluator score this well?"*

Load `references/color-team-checklists.md` for the full checklist. Summary of what Red Team does:

### Step 1 — Compliance check

Walk the compliance matrix end-to-end. For each requirement:

- Is it addressed somewhere in the proposal?
- Is the addressing section the right one (matches what the RFP asks)?
- Is anything missing?

Flag every miss. Compliance misses get fixed before anything else.

### Step 2 — Win theme check

For each major section (Executive Summary, Methodology, Past Performance):

- Is there a clear win theme?
- Is the win theme anchored to a specific evaluation criterion (Section M or equivalent)?
- Is the win theme supported by concrete proof points (not just claims)?

If a section reads as "[Firm] is great at X" without linking back to what the buyer is evaluating, the win theme is wrong. Rewrite.

### Step 3 — Discriminator check

The Tier 1 discriminators must be visible and named:

- Working sample (if applicable) — referenced in executive summary AND in work samples section
- [Federal Intermediary] $[REDACTED]M federal program past performance (if federal-adjacent)

Tier 2 conditional discriminators ([metro-local], 8(a) once certified) should appear when contextually relevant.

If discriminators are buried, foreground them. If they're missing, add them.

### Step 4 — Language mirror check

Pull the "Mirror Language" column from the compliance matrix. For each phrase, search the proposal:

- Are the buyer's exact phrases present?
- Or did the draft accidentally paraphrase ("operational dashboard" instead of "common operating picture")?

Flag every miss. Replace paraphrases with the RFP's exact terminology.

### Step 5 — CCAR defensibility check

For every past performance entry:

- Is every specific number defensible? (dollar amounts, project sizes, dates, %)
- Are reference contacts verified willing?
- Could a reference call contradict any claim?

Flag every uncertainty. CCARs that can't survive a reference call must be reworded to remove the uncertain detail or pulled entirely.

### Step 6 — Voice and AI-tell check

Run the proposal through the voice rules in `references/voice-and-tone.md`:

- Any banned phrases? ("delve," "leverage," "unlock," "comprehensive," "game-changer," "X isn't just about Y")
- Passive voice clusters that should be active?
- Sentence-structure monotony (all sentences the same length)?
- Missing contractions?
- "X is not just Y" or "Y is not just X" patterns?

Flag every instance. Rewrite or excise.

### Step 7 — Red team output

Provide [Principal] a marked-up version of the proposal or a structured list of findings:

- **Compliance gaps:** [list with section references]
- **Win theme weaknesses:** [list with section + rewrite suggestion]
- **Discriminator visibility issues:** [list]
- **Language mirror misses:** [list with RFP phrase vs. draft phrase]
- **CCAR defensibility flags:** [list with `[VERIFY: ...]` items]
- **Voice/AI-tell findings:** [list with sentence + suggested rewrite]
- **Estimated impact:** which fixes are critical (do or fail) vs. nice-to-have

Rank fixes by impact. If [Principal] has limited time, he should fix critical findings first.

## Gold Team Review — executive polish

**When:** Within 24-48 hours of submission. Draft is final-or-nearly-final. Only small edits expected.

**Question this review answers:** *"Is this submission-ready?"*

### Step 1 — Format and submission check

- Page limits respected per section
- Font and spacing per RFP requirements
- Margins per requirements
- File format correct (PDF vs. portal upload vs. multiple files)
- File naming convention per RFP if specified
- Single file vs. multi-file per RFP
- All attachments included and signed
- Submission method correct (email vs. portal)
- Submission addresses correct (CO, alternate contact)
- Deadline confirmed in correct timezone

### Step 2 — Final compliance pass

Walk the compliance matrix one more time. Anything still flagged "not started" or "in progress" gets resolved or removed.

### Step 3 — Cover page and executive summary final read

These are the first things evaluators see. They need to be tight.

- Cover page: every field populated, no placeholders, contact info correct
- Executive summary: opens with buyer problem, names discriminators, fee and timeline visible, no AI tells, fits within page limit

### Step 4 — References verification

- Every reference contacted and confirmed willing
- Names, titles, phone numbers, email addresses correct
- No placeholders

### Step 5 — Signature and certification check

- Debarment certification signed and dated
- Any required reps & certs completed
- Authorized signatory has actual binding authority

### Step 6 — Final read aloud

Read the executive summary, key methodology paragraphs, and past performance entries aloud. Any sentence that sounds wrong gets flagged for one final edit.

### Step 7 — Gold team output

Short checklist for [Principal]:

- ✅ Format compliant
- ✅ All required sections present
- ✅ All attachments signed and included
- ✅ References verified
- ✅ No placeholders remaining
- ✅ Submission method confirmed
- ✅ Deadline and timezone confirmed
- ⚠️ [Any remaining items]

If any item is ⚠️, surface it explicitly with what needs to happen and by when.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Don't soften findings to spare feelings.** Red team's job is to surface problems. If the executive summary is weak, say it's weak.
- **Don't rewrite everything in your own voice.** Edit for the rules — voice, compliance, win themes — not for personal style.
- **Don't skip the language mirror check.** This is the single highest-ROI part of Red Team. Buyers notice when their words come back at them.
- **Don't run Red Team and Gold Team on the same day.** [Principal] needs time between reviews to act on findings. Red Team at draft-60%, Gold Team within 24-48h of submit.
- **Don't pass a proposal with placeholder references.** Three real names, three verified contacts, every time.
- **Don't pass a proposal with `[VERIFY:]` flags still in it.** Every flag gets resolved before submit.
